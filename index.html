Australian Mineral and Economic Contribution

Australia Mining Commodity Distribution

In Australia's Critical Minerals Strategy 2023, the country is committed to harnessing the country's abundant mineral resources for renewable energy and industrial electrification, thereby driving a global shift to “Net Zero Emissions Net Zero Emissions”. As a major producer of lithium, nickel, cobalt and rare earth elements (REEs), Australia supplies key components for batteries, electric vehicles (EVs), energy storage systems, wind turbines and electric vehicle motors.

Mineral exploration expenditure ($m)

In the second quarter of June 2024, the expenditure allocated for exploration activities ascended to $1.01 billion, signifying an 11% augmentation in comparison to the preceding quarter. This trend underscores an escalating commitment to investments in resource exploration, especially in geographic locales such as Queensland and Western Australia, where base metals including nickel, silver, and zinc experienced a notable intensification of exploratory endeavors.

Mineral exploration drill meters (m)

The total meters drilled for mineral exploration in the second quarter of June 2024 amounted to 1.6 million meters, reflecting a 10% increase from the previous quarter. This growth is indicative of the heightened exploration activities in the mining sector, particularly in regions such as Western Australia and Queensland, where the exploration of base metals and precious metals has been a focal point.
